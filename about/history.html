Our History

The San Joaquin Valley Paleontologist Club was founded in 1950 by a small group of fossil enthusiasts, educators, and researchers who shared a passion for uncovering the deep history of life on Earth. What began as informal gatherings in Fresno quickly grew into a thriving organization dedicated to paleontology and science education.

Over the decades, the Club has hosted lectures, field trips, and community events that highlight discoveries from the Cretaceous seas to the Ice Age plains. Members have collaborated with universities, museums, and local schools to bring fossils and evolutionary science to the public, inspiring generations of students and amateur scientists.

In the 1970s, the Club expanded its reach by organizing annual symposia, inviting nationally recognized paleontologists to share their research. These events helped establish the San Joaquin Valley as a hub for scientific dialogue and discovery. Today, the Club continues to celebrate the wonders of paleontology, connecting professional researchers with curious minds across the Valley.

As we mark our 75th year, we remain committed to our founding vision: to explore, preserve, and share the fossil record, ensuring that the stories of ancient life continue to inspire future generations.
