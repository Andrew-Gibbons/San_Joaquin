Board of Directors

The San Joaquin Valley Paleontologist Club is guided by a dedicated Board of Directors whose combined 35 years of service have shaped the organization into a thriving hub for scientific discovery and community engagement. Their leadership ensures that the Club remains true to its mission of exploring, preserving, and sharing the fossil record.

Our board is composed of educators, researchers, and community leaders who bring diverse expertise to the table. Some members have backgrounds in vertebrate paleontology, while others specialize in paleoecology, museum curation, or science education. Together, they provide a balanced perspective that strengthens both our academic programming and our outreach efforts.

Over the past three and a half decades, the Board has overseen countless initiatives: organizing annual lecture series, coordinating field trips to fossil sites, and developing partnerships with universities and museums. Their collective experience has allowed the Club to adapt to changing times while maintaining a strong commitment to scientific integrity and public education.

Board members have also played a crucial role in mentoring the next generation of paleontologists. Through student symposia, internships, and volunteer opportunities, they have opened doors for young scientists eager to learn the craft of excavation, analysis, and storytelling. Their guidance has helped cultivate a vibrant community of learners who carry forward the Club’s legacy.

As we celebrate 75 years of the Club’s history, we honor the Board of Directors for their steadfast leadership. Their combined 35 years of service reflect not only dedication to paleontology but also a deep commitment to the San Joaquin Valley community. With their vision and experience, the Club continues to thrive as a place where science and curiosity meet.
