Unearthing Life from Deep Time

October
David Brancaccio

What can fossilized bones and ancient ecosystems reveal about the history of life on Earth? According to senior paleontologist David Brancaccio, the answers lie in the strata of deep time. Join us at the Saroyan when Brancaccio—an accomplished researcher and storyteller—shares insights from decades of fieldwork and analysis, connecting prehistoric worlds to the challenges of the modern age.

David Brancaccio is a senior paleontologist whose career spans more than three decades of excavation, teaching, and publication. Trained in zoology and earth sciences, he has led major fossil digs across North America, Africa, and Asia, uncovering evidence of species evolution, extinction events, and ecological transformations that shaped the planet’s biodiversity.

Brancaccio's research focuses on vertebrate paleontology, evolutionary biology, and the reconstruction of ancient environments. His work has illuminated how fossil remains—from dinosaur skeletons to microfossils—reflect the dynamics of adaptation, climate change, and survival. He has collaborated with international teams, mentoring younger scientists while advancing comparative studies of prehistoric ecosystems across continents.

As the author of numerous scholarly articles and books, Brancaccio has contributed significantly to the field's understanding of how paleontology informs contemporary debates about evolution and environmental change. He has lectured widely at universities, museums, and public forums, inspiring audiences with his ability to connect the distant past to present-day issues. His career exemplifies how paleontology not only reconstructs ancient worlds but also deepens our understanding of life’s resilience and diversity.
