Andrew Sorkin

Unearthing Stories from Deep Time

April
Andrew Sorkin

What can fossilized bones and ancient ecosystems tell us about the history of life on Earth? According to junior paleontologist Andrew Sorkin, even the smallest discoveries can reveal extraordinary stories of adaptation and survival. Join us at the Saroyan when Sorkin—an emerging voice in the field—shares how early fieldwork experiences are shaping his understanding of prehistoric worlds and their connections to our present environment.

Andrew Sorkin is a junior paleontologist whose research bridges excavation practice with evolutionary interpretation. Born and raised with a fascination for natural history, he studied biology and earth sciences before beginning hands-on fossil fieldwork. His early projects have focused on vertebrate remains in North America, examining how skeletal structures reflect climate shifts, migration, and ecological change.

As part of collaborative research teams, Sorkin has participated in digs across the western United States and Europe, contributing to studies of dinosaur morphology, ancient ecosystems, and extinction patterns. His interests include the paleontology of identity within species, the role of environment in shaping evolutionary pathways, and the ways in which small-scale fossil finds contribute to broader narratives of life’s history.

Sorkin is currently developing his first set of published papers, highlighting lessons learned from fieldwork and the importance of mentorship in paleontology. He has presented at student symposia and community forums, where his enthusiasm for connecting past and present continues to inspire audiences. As he advances in his career, Sorkin’s work promises to shed light on how paleontology helps us understand not only ancient species but also the resilience of life itself.
